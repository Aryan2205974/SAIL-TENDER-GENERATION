# NOTICE INVITING TENDER

## Bid Opening

1. The Techno-Commercial bids shall be opened on the date and time specified in the tender schedule by the designated Bid Opening Committee in the presence of authorized representatives of the bidders who may choose to attend.
2. Following the evaluation of Techno-Commercial bids, SAIL shall notify the eligible bidders whose Techno-Commercial bids have been accepted, and shall then proceed to open their Price Bids.
3. The Price Bids of only those bidders who are found techno-commercially qualified shall be opened. The date and time for the opening of Price Bids shall be communicated separately to the shortlisted bidders.
4. Authorized representatives of the bidders attending the Price Bid opening must carry a valid authorization letter from their respective organizations, duly signed by a competent authority, along with their identity proof.
5. Any bid received after the stipulated submission deadline shall not be considered for opening and shall be returned to the bidder unopened.
6. SAIL reserves the right to postpone or reschedule the bid opening date and time, and such revised schedule shall be communicated to the bidders through appropriate means.
7. The decision of the Bid Opening Committee regarding the qualification of bidders and the opening of bids shall be final and binding.

## Bid Submission

1. Bidders shall submit their bids electronically through the SAIL e-procurement portal, strictly adhering to the stipulated submission deadline of 14.03.2026 at 14:00:00 hours.
2. The bid shall be submitted in two parts: Part-I (Techno-Commercial Bid) and Part-II (Price Bid), both to be uploaded on the e-procurement portal.
3. Bidders are required to upload all supporting documents, including but not limited to GSTIN, PAN, EPF registration, ESI registration, UDYAM Certificate, and Turnover Certificates, duly authenticated and valid as on the date of tender opening.
4. The Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) of Rs. 500,000 shall be submitted in the prescribed manner as detailed in the tender documents, failing which the bid shall be considered non-responsive.
5. Bidders claiming exemption from EMD submission must ensure that all three stipulated clauses for exemption, as mentioned in the tender document, are fulfilled; otherwise, the offer may be cancelled.
6. All bids submitted must remain valid for a period of 120 days from the date of price discovery, as specified in the tender conditions.
7. Bidders shall ensure that their submitted bids are free from any erasures or overwriting, and any corrections must be duly authenticated by the bidder.
8. Any bid received after the stipulated submission deadline shall be summarily rejected and returned to the bidder unopened.
