**FORCE MAJEURE**  

1. **Definition**: Force Majeure shall mean any event beyond the reasonable control of either party, including but not limited to acts of God, war, riots, strikes, lockouts, government restrictions, natural disasters, or any other unforeseeable circumstances preventing contractual obligations.  

2. **Notification**: The affected party shall notify the other party in writing within seven (7) days of the occurrence of such event, providing details and expected duration of the disruption.  

3. **Mitigation**: The affected party shall take all reasonable steps to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure event and resume performance as soon as practicable.  

4. **Extension of Time**: If performance is delayed due to Force Majeure, the contract duration shall be extended proportionately without penalty, subject to mutual agreement.  

5. **Termination**: If the Force Majeure event persists beyond a period of ninety (90) days, either party may terminate the contract without liability, except for obligations accrued prior to the event.  

6. **Proof of Force Majeure**: The affected party shall submit documentary evidence from competent authorities substantiating the claim.  

7. **Exclusions**: Failure to secure permits, financial constraints, or market fluctuations shall not constitute Force Majeure.  

8. **Continued Obligations**: Obligations not affected by the Force Majeure event shall remain enforceable.  

9. **Dispute Resolution**: Any disputes arising from Force Majeure shall be resolved as per the arbitration clause of the contract.  

10. **Applicability**: This clause shall be governed by the General Conditions of Contract (SAIL – S1) unless superseded by Special Conditions.
