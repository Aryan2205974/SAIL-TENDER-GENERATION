**SUBSECTION: TERMINATION**  

1. The Contract may be terminated by either party by issuing a written notice of at least thirty (30) days, specifying the reasons for termination.  

2. In case of termination due to the Contractor’s default, including but not limited to failure to meet delivery schedules, quality standards, or contractual obligations, SAIL reserves the right to forfeit the Performance Security.  

3. If termination arises due to SAIL’s requirements, the Contractor shall be entitled to payment for work executed up to the termination date, subject to verification.  

4. Force Majeure events, including natural calamities, strikes, or government restrictions, shall entitle either party to terminate the Contract without penalty, provided written notice is furnished within seven (7) days of occurrence.  

5. Upon termination, the Contractor shall hand over all documents, materials, and unfinished work to SAIL as directed, ensuring no disruption to operations.  

6. Any disputes arising from termination shall be resolved as per the arbitration clause under the General Conditions of Contract (SAIL – S1).  

7. The provisions of Special Conditions of Contract shall prevail in case of conflict with General Conditions regarding termination.  

8. SAIL reserves the right to blacklist the Contractor for repeated defaults, subject to due process.  

9. Termination shall not absolve the Contractor of liabilities accrued prior to the termination date.  

10. All obligations under confidentiality and intellectual property clauses shall survive termination.
